## TL;DR

If viewers swipe in the first 2 seconds, your opening is forcing them to **wait**.
Fix it by rewriting the first line into one of these:

1. **Result-first**: say the payoff immediately.
2. **Mistake-first**: call out what beginners do wrong.
3. **Counterintuitive-first**: state an unpopular truth.

(Editing helps later. But the first 2 seconds is almost always **message**, not montage.)

## Problem

A typical “bad” opening looks like this:

- “Hey guys, today I’m going to show you…”
- “So I was thinking about…”
- “Let’s talk about…”

These openings create friction because they delay the reward.
In Shorts, the viewer is already holding the “skip” button.

**The real problem:** your hook doesn’t answer the viewer’s only question:

> “Why should I keep watching *right now*?”

## The simple fix

### Step 1: Identify what your first line is doing

Read your first on-screen line (or spoken line) and label it:

- **Intro** (bad for Shorts)
- **Context** (sometimes OK, usually too slow)
- **Payoff** (best)

If it’s Intro/Context, swap it.

### Step 2: Swap the first line using one of the 3 hook swaps

#### Swap A) Result-first (Payoff in 1 sentence)

Format:

- **Get/Do/Build [Result] in [Time] (without [Pain]).**

Why it works: it compresses the reward into a single promise.

#### Swap B) Mistake-first (Pattern interrupt)

Format:

- **If you’re doing [Common thing], that’s why your [Result] is stuck.**

Why it works: it creates “oh no… that’s me” attention.

#### Swap C) Counterintuitive-first (Unpopular truth)

Format:

- **Stop doing [Popular advice]. Do this instead.**

Why it works: it creates tension and curiosity.

## Examples (copy/paste)

Use these as your first on-screen line. Then immediately show proof, a demo, or the first step.

### Result-first examples

- “Write a 30-second Shorts script in 3 minutes (without sounding like AI).”
- “Get your first 1,000 views in 24 hours (even with 0 followers).”
- “Turn 1 idea into 10 Shorts in 20 minutes (with a simple template).”

### Mistake-first examples

- “If your hook starts with ‘Hey guys’, that’s why people swipe.”
- “If you explain the topic first, you’re killing retention before it starts.”
- “If you wait to reveal the result, you’re making viewers do work.”

### Counterintuitive-first examples

- “Your editing isn’t the problem. Your first sentence is.”
- “Stop teasing the topic. Show the payoff first.”
- “Don’t ‘introduce’ the video. Start with the answer.”

## Common mistakes

1. **Mistaking ‘energy’ for a hook**
   - Loud voice + fast cuts won’t save a weak first line.

2. **Promising something vague**
   - “This will change everything” is not a promise.
   - Make it specific: result, time, audience, or constraint.

3. **Starting with context that belongs at 5 seconds**
   - Put the why/how after you’ve earned attention.

4. **No proof after the hook**
   - Hook = promise.
   - Next line = proof (screen recording, example, result screenshot, first step).

## Next step

Pick one of your recent Shorts and do a 5-minute rewrite sprint:

1. Write **3 alternative first lines** (Result / Mistake / Counterintuitive)
2. Record just the first 3 seconds for each
3. Post the strongest one and reuse the winners as a personal swipe file
