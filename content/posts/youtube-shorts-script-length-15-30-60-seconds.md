If you’re stuck on “how long should my Short be?”, you’re asking the right question.
Script length changes **retention**, **pacing**, and what kind of promise you can make.

Here’s a practical rule set to choose between **15s**, **30s**, and **60s** (plus copy-paste script structures).

## The real goal isn’t length — it’s one clear promise
Before you pick 15/30/60, decide your **one sentence outcome**:

- “You’ll learn 1 thing.”
- “You’ll get 3 examples.”
- “You’ll see a before/after.”

If you can’t say the promise in one sentence, the script will sprawl (and retention dies).

## 15 seconds: one idea, one punch
**Best for:** quick tips, simple mistakes, single hook formula, one tool shortcut.

### What works in 15s
- **1 hook line**
- **1 core point**
- **1 micro-proof** (optional)
- **1 next step**

### 15s script template
1) Hook (1–2s)
> “If your Shorts die at 2 seconds, it’s usually this.”

2) Core point (8–10s)
> “Your first line is talking about you. Flip it: talk about the viewer’s result.”

3) Next step (2–3s)
> “Rewrite your hook as: ‘Get X in Y minutes without Z.’”

**Tip:** 15s is unforgiving. Cut every adjective.

## 30 seconds: one promise + 3 bullets
**Best for:** mini frameworks, 3 examples, step-by-step micro tutorial.

### What works in 30s
- Hook
- Promise
- **3 bullets** (fast)
- CTA (soft)

### 30s script template
1) Hook (1–2s)
> “Here are 3 hooks that increase retention instantly.”

2) Promise (1s)
> “Copy these and plug in your niche.”

3) 3 bullets (20–22s)
> “1) ‘Stop doing X if you want Y.’
> 2) ‘The 10-second fix for X.’
> 3) ‘I tried X for 7 days — here’s what happened.’”

4) CTA (3–5s)
> “Comment ‘HOOK’ and I’ll reply with the template.”

**Tip:** If your 30s script has more than 3 bullets, it’s not a 30s script anymore.

## 60 seconds: story, tension, payoff
**Best for:** case studies, story-based lessons, comparisons, “I tested X” videos.

### What works in 60s
- Hook + context
- Tension (what went wrong / what was missing)
- Steps or story beats
- Result + takeaway

### 60s script structure (story-based)
- Hook: “I fixed my Shorts retention with one change.”
- Context: what the viewer should care about
- Tension: the mistake / constraint
- Attempt: what you changed
- Result: numbers or qualitative outcome
- Lesson: what to copy

**Tip:** 60s needs **payoff**. If there’s no result or reveal, keep it 30s.

## A simple decision tree (use this)
Choose your length like this:

- Can this be explained in **one sentence + one example**?
  - Yes → **15s**
- Does it need **3 bullets** or **a quick tutorial**?
  - Yes → **30s**
- Does it need **context + tension + result**?
  - Yes → **60s**

If you’re still unsure, default to **30 seconds**.
It’s the best balance of clarity and depth.
