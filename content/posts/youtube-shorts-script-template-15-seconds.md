If your Shorts feel "random," it’s usually because the script doesn’t have a *clear micro-structure*.

Here’s a simple template you can reuse for **15 seconds** that works especially well for tips, mini-tutorials, and quick opinions.

## The template (15 seconds)

### 0–2s: Hook (one sentence)
Aim for **specific curiosity**.

Pick one:
- **Outcome-first:** “Do this to get ___ in ___.”
- **Mistake-first:** “Stop doing ___ if you want ___.”
- **Counterintuitive:** “The reason ___ doesn’t work is ___.”

### 2–10s: Proof (2 beats)
Give *just enough* to earn attention.

Use:
- **Beat 1:** the key idea (one line)
- **Beat 2:** the example (one line)

Example:
- Beat 1: “Shorts need a visible change every ~2 seconds.”
- Beat 2: “So cut the silence and show the result first, then the method.”

### 10–15s: CTA (one clear action)
Keep it non-needy and specific:
- “Try this on your next post and save the draft.”
- “Comment ‘TEMPLATE’ and I’ll send the structure.”
- “If you want the Notion workflow, grab the OS below.”

## Fill-in-the-blank version
Copy/paste:

1) Hook: “If you’re trying to ___, stop ___.”
2) Proof beat 1: “Instead, do ___ because ___.”
3) Proof beat 2: “For example: ___.”
4) CTA: “Now try ___ on your next Short.”
