If you sell a Notion template on Gumroad, your product page is basically your landing page *and* your SEO page.
You don’t need fancy tools to improve it—just a few deliberate edits.

## 1) Put the keyword in the first line
Many buyers (and search engines) only see the beginning.
Start with a clear promise:

> “A Notion system to plan and script YouTube Shorts in 30 minutes.”

## 2) Use a clear naming pattern
Aim for: **Outcome + Format + Audience**

Examples:
- “30-Day Shorts Challenge Planner (Notion Template)”
- “AI Script Writing Cheat Sheet (Shorts)”

## 3) Write like a scanner
Use short paragraphs and bold the important bits:
- **What you get** (exact files/pages)
- **Who it’s for**
- **How long setup takes**

## 4) Match your screenshots to your promise
Screenshots should show the *outcome*, not the UI.
Good screenshot checklist:
- a dashboard view
- one example page filled in
- one database filtered/sorted for the “aha” moment

## 5) Add a tiny FAQ (3 questions max)
Good FAQ reduces refund anxiety:
- “Do I need Notion paid?”
- “Can I duplicate instantly?”
- “How fast can I set this up?”

## 6) Add one honest constraint
Trust goes up when you’re specific:
- “This won’t write the script for you—but it will make shipping consistent.”

## 7) Link your products like a bundle (without bundling)
If your products are related, cross-link them in the description.
It helps buyers navigate and helps your internal traffic.
