If you sell a Notion template on Gumroad, your #1 job is reducing friction.

Most creators overcomplicate delivery:
- long setup docs
- screenshots
- step-by-step “click here” instructions

But the best buyer experience is usually just one thing:

**A single Notion Duplicate link.**

This post explains how to ship that cleanly (and what to avoid).

## The ideal buyer flow
Your buyer should be able to do this in under 2 minutes:

1) click the Gumroad download link
2) open the Notion page
3) click **Duplicate**
4) land in their workspace with everything ready

If your product needs 10+ minutes of setup, you’ll see more refunds and more “how do I install this?” emails.

## What to deliver (minimum viable)
For most Notion template products, deliver:

- **Notion Duplicate link** (the actual template)
- a short **README** with:
  - “How to duplicate” (1 paragraph)
  - “What you get” (bullet list)
  - troubleshooting (3 bullets max)

Optional:
- a **CSV import kit** if you want to provide sample rows without manually duplicating databases

## How to create a proper Duplicate link
Notion supports a dedicated duplicate URL format.

Use this structure:

`https://your-site.notion.site/your-page?duplicate=1`

Notes:
- Use the **public share URL** (not an internal workspace URL).
- Confirm the page is set to **Share to web**.
- Open the link in an incognito window to verify it works for non-logged-in users.

## Common mistakes (that break delivery)
### 1) Shipping an internal notion.so link
Internal links (workspace links) often fail for buyers.

Fix: always use the public `*.notion.site` URL.

### 2) Overloading the product with setup docs
A long setup guide is usually a symptom that the template isn’t “copy-ready”.

Fix: simplify the template until duplication is enough.

### 3) Promising files you don’t actually include
Example:
- your Gumroad page says “includes 20 prompts”
- but the download only has 10

Fix: keep a single source of truth for your “What you get” list, and verify before publishing.

## A simple Gumroad description snippet you can copy
Use something like:

- Instant download
- Includes a Notion template (one-click duplicate link)
- Setup time: ~2 minutes
- Works on Notion free plan

Then add the duplicate link inside the download file (README), not directly in the public description.
