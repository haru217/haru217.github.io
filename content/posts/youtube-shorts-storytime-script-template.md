Storytime Shorts are one of the easiest formats to *finish*…
and one of the easiest formats to mess up.

Most creators either:
- ramble (no structure),
- hide the conflict too long,
- or end with “so yeah…” (no payoff).

Use this **plug-and-play story template** to keep retention high.

## The template (45–60 seconds)

### 0–2s: Cold open (the “problem headline”)
Start inside the tension.

Pick one:
- “I almost lost ___ because I did ___.”
- “This is the mistake that cost me ___.”
- “I thought ___ would work, and it completely backfired.”

### 2–6s: Stakes (why it matters)
One sentence.

Examples:
- “If this failed, I’d have to ___.”
- “And I had only ___ hours to fix it.”

### 6–20s: Context (just enough)
2–3 short beats. Don’t explain everything.

- Beat 1: what you were trying to do
- Beat 2: what you did (the key action)
- Beat 3: what went wrong (the turning point)

### 20–35s: The twist (the surprising detail)
This is what earns the rewatch.

Examples:
- “The problem wasn’t ___… it was ___.”
- “Then I noticed one tiny thing: ___.”

### 35–50s: Resolution (what you changed)
Make the fix concrete.

Options:
- “I removed ___ and replaced it with ___.”
- “I did ___ first, then ___.”

### 50–58s: Lesson (one clean rule)
Turn the story into a repeatable takeaway.

- “Rule: If ___, do ___.”
- “The shortcut is: ___.”

### 58–60s: CTA (one action)
Keep it low friction:
- “Save this template and use it on your next story Short.”
- “Comment ‘STORY’ and I’ll reply with the fill-in version.”
- “If you want my full Shorts workflow, it’s linked below.”
