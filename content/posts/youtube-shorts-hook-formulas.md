Most Shorts don’t fail because the content is bad.
They fail because **the first 1–2 seconds don’t make a clear promise**.

Below are **7 hook formulas** you can reuse. Each one includes fill‑in‑the‑blank + examples.

## 1) Result in time (Outcome-first)
**Formula:** “Get **{result}** in **{time}** (without **{common pain}**).”

Examples:
- “Get clean captions in 30 seconds (without editing in Premiere).”
- “Get 10 content ideas in 2 minutes (without staring at a blank page).”

## 2) Stop doing this (Mistake-first)
**Formula:** “Stop **{mistake}** if you want **{desired outcome}**.”

Examples:
- “Stop starting with ‘Hey guys’ if you want higher retention.”
- “Stop explaining first if you want more saves.”

## 3) Do this instead (Swap)
**Formula:** “Don’t **{bad approach}**. Do **{better approach}**.”

Examples:
- “Don’t write scripts top-to-bottom. Write the hook last.”
- “Don’t open with context. Open with the payoff.”

## 4) The reason it’s not working (Root cause)
**Formula:** “The reason **{thing}** isn’t working is **{root cause}**.”

Examples:
- “The reason your Shorts feel ‘boring’ is there’s no visible change.”
- “The reason your hooks miss is they don’t name the audience.”

## 5) Tiny rule (One principle)
**Formula:** “One rule for **{topic}**: **{principle}**.”

Examples:
- “One rule for Shorts: change something every 2 seconds.”
- “One rule for scripts: one idea per video.”

## 6) Quick test (Interactive)
**Formula:** “Quick test: if you can’t answer **{question}**, your hook is weak.”

Examples:
- “Quick test: if you can’t finish ‘This video is for…’, your hook is weak.”
- “Quick test: if the viewer can’t predict the payoff in 1 second, rewrite.”

## 7) The 3-part promise (Who + outcome + proof)
**Formula:** “If you’re **{who}**, here’s how to **{outcome}**. I’ll show you **{proof/steps}**.”

Examples:
- “If you’re a solo creator, here’s how to batch Shorts scripts. I’ll show you the 3-step Notion workflow.”
- “If you’re stuck at 200 views, here’s how to fix your hooks. I’ll show you 5 rewrites.”

---

## Hook checklist (save this)
A good hook usually has at least 2 of these:
- **Audience** (who it’s for)
- **Outcome** (what changes)
- **Timeframe** (how fast)
- **Constraint** (without X)
- **Proof** (why believe you)
