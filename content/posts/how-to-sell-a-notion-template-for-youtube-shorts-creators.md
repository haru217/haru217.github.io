Selling a Notion template is not about “having a pretty dashboard.”
It’s about solving one *specific* bottleneck for one *specific* creator.

This guide is the exact workflow I’d use to sell a Notion template for **YouTube Shorts creators**—from idea → template → Gumroad page → traffic.

## Step 1) Pick a single Shorts bottleneck (not “everything”)
If your template does too much, it’s hard to explain and hard to buy.
Pick one:

- **Ideas → Scripts workflow** (capture ideas, turn them into scripts fast)
- **30-day posting plan** (daily prompts + calendar)
- **Analytics + review** (track metrics, learn what worked)

If you want an example of the “ideas → scripts” angle, I wrote a deeper workflow post here:
- /posts/notion-batch-youtube-shorts-scripts-workflow/

## Step 2) Build the smallest version that produces an outcome
Your MVP template should answer:

- “What do I do next?”
- “Where do I put things?”
- “How do I finish one Short?”

A practical structure that sells:

1. **Dashboard** (one page)
2. **Ideas DB** (title + hook + status)
3. **Scripts DB** (title + script + status + publish date)
4. **Publish Log** (optional, can be a simple DB)

Related post (calendar-style approach):
- /posts/notion-content-calendar-for-youtube-shorts/

## Step 3) Make duplication effortless (this is conversion)
Most refunds happen because setup is confusing.

Minimum requirements:
- a clean Notion public page
- a **Duplicate** link that works
- a short setup guide (3–7 minutes)

If you’ve never shipped a Notion duplicate link before:
- /posts/notion-template-duplicate-link-gumroad-delivery/

## Step 4) Write the Gumroad page like a “scan-friendly” landing page
Your Gumroad product page *is* your landing page.
Structure it like this:

- **1-line promise** (first line)
- **Who it’s for** / who it’s not for
- **What you get** (exact pages/files)
- **Setup time** + requirements
- **3-question FAQ**

Templates and checklists:
- /posts/gumroad-product-description-template/
- /posts/gumroad-product-page-checklist-for-5-dollar-templates/

## Step 5) Do the basic Gumroad SEO (title + slug + first paragraph)
You don’t need hacks.
You need clarity and consistency.

Start with:
- a keyword-rich title (human-readable)
- a clean URL slug
- the keyword in the first paragraph
