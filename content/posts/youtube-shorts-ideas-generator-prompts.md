If you’re a solo creator, consistency usually doesn’t break because you’re lazy.
It breaks because **you don’t have a reliable way to generate ideas on demand**.

Below are **25 plug‑and‑play prompts** you can reuse every week.
Pick 3–5, batch your scripts, and you’re done.

## How to use these prompts (fast)
1. Choose a **niche + audience** (e.g., “freelance designers”).
2. Answer in **1 sentence**.
3. Turn the sentence into a Short with this structure:
   - Hook (1–2 sec)
   - 3 bullets
   - CTA (one step)

---

## A) Beginner wins (easy, high demand)
1) “If you’re new to {topic}, start with {one step}.”
2) “3 mistakes beginners make in {topic}.”
3) “Do this before you {common action}.”
4) “The fastest way to get {result} (without {pain}).”
5) “One rule that changed my results in {topic}.”

## B) Myth vs truth (instant attention)
6) “Everyone thinks {myth}. The truth is {truth}.”
7) “Stop doing {habit}. Do {better habit} instead.”
8) “You don’t need {tool/thing} to get {result}.”
9) “The reason {thing} isn’t working is {root cause}.”
10) “This ‘best practice’ is overrated: {practice}. Here’s what matters.”

## C) Quick frameworks (save-worthy)
11) “Use this 3-step checklist for {task}: {step1}, {step2}, {step3}.”
12) “The {X}-minute method for {task}.”
13) “If {condition}, then {action}. If not, then {fallback}.”
14) “My template for {deliverable}: {section1}, {section2}, {section3}.”
15) “The only 2 metrics I watch for {goal}.”

## D) Proof + process (trust builder)
16) “Here’s how I go from {start} to {end} in {timeframe}.”
17) “I used to {bad result}. Then I changed {one thing}.”
18) “Behind the scenes: how I batch {content type}.”
19) “I tested {A} vs {B}. Here’s what happened.”
20) “Steal my workflow: {tool1} → {tool2} → {output}.”

## E) Audience call-outs (high relevance)
21) “If you’re {who}, stop {mistake}.”
22) “If you’re stuck at {plateau}, do {next action}.”
23) “If you only have {constraint}, focus on {leverage}.”
24) “If you hate {thing}, try {alternative}.”
25) “If you’re overwhelmed, do this 10-minute reset: {steps}.”

---

## Turn 1 idea into 5 Shorts (repurpose formula)
When one prompt hits, reuse it like this:
- Version 1: “Stop doing {mistake}…”
- Version 2: “Do {better approach} instead…”
- Version 3: “Quick test: if you can’t answer {question}…”
- Version 4: “3-step checklist…”
- Version 5: “Behind the scenes…”
