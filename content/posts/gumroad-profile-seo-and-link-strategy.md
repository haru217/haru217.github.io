If you sell on Gumroad, your **product pages** matter.
But your **profile page** matters too.

Your profile is often the first thing people see when they:

- Click your Gumroad username
- Browse related products and land on your page
- Search your brand name (Google will sometimes show the profile)

This post is a small, practical checklist to make your Gumroad profile work like a “hub” that pushes traffic to your best pages.

## 1) Decide what your profile is *for*
Pick one job for the profile. Here are the common options:

- **Marketplace discovery**: help Gumroad visitors find your products quickly
- **External SEO**: give Google a clean brand page + links
- **Conversion**: route people to your single best product or bundle

If you try to do all three, the page turns into a messy link tree.

A simple default:

- 1–2 lines: who you help + what you sell
- 3 links: (a) best product, (b) free lead magnet, (c) blog

## 2) Use a “searchable one-liner” (not a vibe)
Most bios are too vague.

Bad:
- “Helping creators grow.”

Better:
- “Notion templates and prompt packs for YouTube Shorts creators.”

That second sentence contains real keywords people type.

## 3) Pin the *one* product you want seen first
If you have multiple products, people get choice paralysis.

Pick one:

- Your best seller
- Your highest-converting product
- The product that best represents your brand promise

Then make everything else secondary.

Rule of thumb for low-priced products ($3–$7):

- Choose the product that can be explained in **one sentence**
- Avoid “system” products as the first click if they need lots of context

## 4) Add one external link that builds trust
Gumroad buyers want to know you’re real.

One good external link is enough:

- A blog “Start Here” page
- A public Notion page with a preview
- A simple landing page with screenshots + FAQs

If you link to a blog, make sure the page is:

- Fast
- Mobile-friendly
- Clear in the first screen (headline + 1 paragraph)

## 5) Create a small “link ladder” (profile → page → product)
Here’s a clean flow that avoids dumping people directly into checkout:

1. Gumroad profile (promise + 3 links)
2. Blog post (teaches something + shows credibility)
3. Product page (clear offer)

This works because:

- The blog post *earns attention*
- The product page *converts attention*

## 6) Use the same keywords across profile + product titles
You don’t need keyword stuffing.
You need **consistency**.

Example keyword set:

- “YouTube Shorts”
- “Notion template”
- “hook formulas” / “script templates”

Then:

- Profile bio includes “YouTube Shorts” + “Notion templates”
- Product titles include “YouTube Shorts” + the exact format (“Notion Template”, “PDF”, “Swipe File”)
- Blog post titles include the same phrases

Consistency helps both:

- Humans (they understand what you sell)
- Search engines (they see topical focus)

## 7) Quick profile checklist (copy/paste)
Use this as your profile QA:

- [ ] Bio says **audience + outcome + format**
- [ ] First product shown is the one you want people to buy
- [ ] Profile text matches the keywords in your top product title
- [ ] Exactly 1–3 external links (no link tree chaos)
- [ ] External link page loads fast and explains the offer quickly

## Next step
If you’re building a blog (Hugo + GitHub Pages), create one “Start Here” page that:

- Explains what you sell
- Links to your best 3 posts
- Links to your best product

Then put that page as the main external link on your Gumroad profile.

That’s enough to start compounding traffic.
