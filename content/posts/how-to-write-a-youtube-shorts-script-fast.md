## TL;DR

Use this structure and fill each line in order:

1) **Hook (1 sentence)**
2) **Beat 1 (why it matters)**
3) **Beat 2 (the key idea)**
4) **Beat 3 (the simplest step)**
5) **Payoff (the result / takeaway)**
6) **CTA (optional, 1 short line)**

If you can’t write the Hook in 30 seconds, write the **Payoff** first.

## Problem

Most creators don’t have a “script problem.” They have a **decision problem**:

- too many ways to start
- too many ways to explain
- too many ways to end

So you sit there rewriting the first sentence.

The fix is to **reduce choices** with a one-page template.

## The simple fix (the 5-minute template)

Open a notes app and write these six lines **exactly**:

**HOOK:**

**BEAT 1 (why):**

**BEAT 2 (what):**

**BEAT 3 (how):**

**PAYOFF:**

**CTA (optional):**

Then follow these rules:

- Each Beat is **one sentence**.
- No “intro,” no “context,” no “story” (unless it’s a storytime format).
- Keep the language concrete: numbers, actions, nouns.

## Common mistakes

- **Writing the hook last.** (Your hook is your promise; everything else supports it.)
- **Trying to say two ideas.** One Short = one idea.
- **No payoff.** If you don’t land the result, the video feels like a rant.
- **CTA that hijacks the ending.** Keep it one short line or skip it.
