Most creators don’t fail because they “lack ideas.”
They fail because they **don’t have a repeatable planning system**.

A content calendar doesn’t need to be fancy.
It just needs to do 3 jobs:

1. Make it obvious what to post today
2. Reduce decision fatigue
3. Create momentum (so you ship even when motivation dips)

Here’s a simple **30-day YouTube Shorts content calendar** you can set up in 20 minutes.

## The 30-day structure (keep it stupid-simple)

Use this weekly loop 4 times:

- **Mon**: Teach 1 thing (how-to)
- **Tue**: Myth-bust (hot take / contrarian)
- **Wed**: “3 mistakes” / “3 tips” list
- **Thu**: Quick case study (before/after)
- **Fri**: Tools/workflow (your process)
- **Sat**: Personal story (lesson learned)
- **Sun**: Q&A (answer 1 common comment)

Why it works:
- you always know the *format*
- your brain only has to pick the *topic*

## Step 1: Create one table with 5 columns

You can do this in Notion, Google Sheets, or even a paper notebook.

Columns:
- **Day** (1–30)
- **Format** (Teach / Myth / List / Case / Workflow / Story / Q&A)
- **Topic** (one line)
- **Hook** (first sentence)
- **Status** (Planned / Scripted / Posted)

That’s it.

## Step 2: Fill the first 10 days only

Don’t plan all 30 days up front.
Planning is a form of procrastination if it becomes a “big project.”

Do this instead:

- Write 10 topics (one line each)
- Write 10 hooks (one line each)

Then start posting.

When you reach Day 7, refill the next 7 days.

## Step 3: Use a hook formula so you never get stuck

Pick one and reuse it:

- **“Stop doing X. Do Y instead.”**
- **“If you only remember 1 thing about {topic}, it’s this…”**
- **“3 mistakes that kill your Shorts in the first 2 seconds”**
- **“The fastest way to get {result} (without {pain})”**

Your goal is not to be clever.
Your goal is to be *clear*.

## Step 4: Add one rule to make it sustainable

Use this rule for the entire 30 days:

**No rewriting scripts after you start recording.**

If the hook is clear and the next 2–3 points are obvious, record it.
Shipping beats polishing.

## A tiny workflow that compounds

Daily (10 minutes):
- pick today’s topic
- write a hook
- write 2–3 bullet points

Weekly (30 minutes):
- refill the next 7 days
- review which formats performed best

That’s how you go from “random posting” to **consistent output**.
