## TL;DR

If you don’t know what to say next while writing Shorts, use this:

1) **Hook** (0–2s): make a clear promise
2) **Value** (2–18s): 2–3 specific steps or bullets
3) **Payoff** (18–25s): proof, result, or mini-demo
4) **CTA** (25–30s): one simple next action

## Problem

Most Shorts die because the script is either:

- a long intro (no promise)
- a list of tips (no payoff)
- a “trust me bro” claim (no proof)
- a confusing CTA (too many options)

The fix isn’t “be more creative.” It’s **a tighter structure**.

## The simple fix

Write every Short using the same 4 blocks:

### 1) Hook (0–2s)

A hook is a **promise** + **who it’s for** + **timeframe**.

Template:

> **Get [result] in [time] even if you’re [pain point].**

Examples:

- Get a clean Short script in 5 minutes even if you hate writing.
- Fix your first 2 seconds today even if your views are stuck.

### 2) Value (2–18s)

Deliver the “how” as 2–3 bullets. Keep each bullet **one sentence**.

Rules:

- Use numbers (2 steps, 3 mistakes, 1 checklist)
- Use strong verbs (cut, swap, add, remove)
- Avoid theory—give actions

### 3) Payoff (18–25s)

Show proof. Options:

- a quick before/after line
- one specific example
- a tiny demo (screen, list, visual)

### 4) CTA (25–30s)

Pick **one** action:

- Follow for more
- Comment a keyword (“SCRIPT”) and I’ll share the template
- Save this

Avoid: “Like, follow, comment, share, and check my link.”

## Common mistakes

- Writing the hook last (write it first)
- Adding more bullets instead of adding proof
- Saying “stay tuned” instead of giving a result
- Switching topics mid-Short
