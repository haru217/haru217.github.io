Consistency beats motivation.
But most creators fail at consistency because their “plan” lives in their head.

Here’s a simple Notion setup that makes YouTube Shorts publishing feel automatic — without turning your workflow into a full-time job.

## The minimal database you need
Create one database with these properties:

- **Title** (title)
- **Status** (select): Idea / Draft / Filming / Posted
- **Publish date** (date)
- **Hook** (text)
- **CTA** (text)
- **Notes** (text)

That’s it. No fancy dashboards required.

## The 30-day plan (in one view)
Make a Calendar view and block 30 days ahead.

Rule of thumb:
- **Weekdays:** educational / how-to / list style
- **Weekends:** story / opinion / behind-the-scenes

If you can’t fill 30 titles today, fill 10.
Then add 1–2 new ideas per day.

## The “Hook first” workflow
Most people write the script first.
Flip it.

For each day, write:
1) Hook
2) 3 bullets (what you’ll say)
3) CTA

You’ll never stare at a blank page again.

## The only KPI that matters at the start
Track one number per post:

- **3-second hold rate** (did they keep watching after the hook?)

If the hook fails, everything fails.
