If you sell a $3–$7 digital product, your Gumroad page has one job:
**make the buyer say “yes” fast**—and also be readable by search.

Here’s a simple checklist you can run in 15 minutes.

## 1) Title = Outcome + Format + Audience
Bad: “Notion Template v2”

Good patterns:
- “30-Day Shorts Challenge Planner (Notion Template)”
- “Viral Hook Swipe File (50 Hooks for Shorts)”

This helps both:
- humans scanning
- Google understanding what the page is about

## 2) First 2 lines: your main keyword + promise
Don’t start with fluff.

Write a clear first line like:
> “A Notion planner to publish 30 YouTube Shorts in 30 days.”

Then add one sentence of proof:
> “Follow one prompt per day—no dashboards, no overthinking.”

## 3) Add a tight “What you get” section
Make it concrete.

Use bullets and list **exact items**:
- 1 Notion dashboard
- 1 database (Day 1 → Day 30)
- 5 idea prompts

If it’s a file product:
- `hooks_50.csv`
- `README.pdf`

## 4) Add “Who it’s for” (and one honest “Not for”)
This qualifies the buyer and reduces refunds.

Example:
- For: solo creators who want consistency
- Not for: people who want automated growth or guaranteed views

## 5) Put the setup time in writing
A tiny line that removes anxiety:
- “Setup time: ~2 minutes (duplicate link)”
- “Reading time: ~5 minutes”

## 6) Use 3–5 screenshots with a story
Aim for:
1) the dashboard / main view
2) an example page filled in (so it feels real)
3) the “aha” view (filtered/sorted)

Avoid UI-only screenshots that don’t show outcomes.

## 7) Add a micro-FAQ (3 questions)
Steal these:
- “Do I need Notion paid?”
- “Can I duplicate instantly?”
- “Is this a course?”

## 8) Cross-link related products (internal traffic)
At the bottom, add:
- “If you liked this, you might also like: {other product link}”

This increases session depth even if you have low external traffic.

## 9) One-line SEO summary (for you)
Before you publish, answer:
- “What would someone type to find this?”

Then ensure that phrase appears in:
- title
- first paragraph
- one subheading

---

If you want a quick starting point, copy this mini-structure:

- First line: keyword + promise
- Who it’s for
- What you get
- How to use
- Outcomes
- FAQ
- Related links
