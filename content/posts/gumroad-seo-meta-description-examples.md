If you’re doing SEO for Gumroad, most people obsess over keywords… and then waste their clicks with a weak snippet.

Your **meta description** (and the first 1–2 lines of your product description) often becomes the text Google shows under your title.
That snippet is your *ad copy*.

Below are **copy-paste patterns** you can adapt for:
- Notion templates
- prompt packs
- cheat sheets

## First: what Gumroad actually gives you
Gumroad doesn’t let you set a separate “meta description” field like WordPress.
In practice, Google often pulls from:
- your product description
- sometimes your profile text

So your job is simple:
**make the first 160–300 characters of your product description do real work.**

## The 5 best meta-description patterns (with examples)

### 1) Outcome + who it’s for + time-to-value
Use this when your product delivers a clear result fast.

**Template**
> {Outcome} for {who}. Get set up in {time}. Includes {deliverable}.

**Example (Notion template)**
> Plan and publish YouTube Shorts faster for solo creators. Set up in 5 minutes. Includes a Notion dashboard + script templates.

**Example (prompt pack)**
> Write better Shorts scripts for busy creators. Start in 2 minutes. Includes 20 copy-ready prompts + 5 hook formulas.

### 2) Pain → promise → proof (micro)
Use this when the reader feels stuck and needs reassurance.

**Template**
> Stuck on {pain}? Use this {asset} to {promise}. Built for {who}. Instant download.

**Example**
> Stuck writing hooks? Use this swipe file to craft stronger openings in minutes. Built for YouTube Shorts creators. Instant download.

### 3) Checklist / kit positioning (high intent)
Use this when the buyer wants “everything in one place.”

**Template**
> A complete {kit} for {job}. Includes {items}. Works with {tool}. Instant access.

**Example**
> A complete content factory kit for YouTube Shorts. Includes templates, prompts, and CSV import files. Works with Notion. Instant access.

### 4) Specific deliverable count (only if it’s real)
Numbers increase CTR, but only if they’re accurate.

**Template**
> Includes {#} {deliverable} + {#} {deliverable}. {Outcome}. Instant download.

**Example**
> Includes 50 hook templates + 10 niche categories. Write stronger first lines and improve retention. Instant download.

### 5) “Not for everyone” qualifier (filters bad buyers)
Use this to reduce refunds and attract the right people.

**Template**
> For {who}. Not for {who-not}. Get {outcome} with {asset}.

**Example**
> For solo creators who publish weekly. Not for people who want a done-for-you channel. Get a repeatable Shorts workflow in Notion.

## The 7 rules that make these work

1) **Lead with the outcome** (not features)
2) **Say who it’s for** (creator niche, skill level)
3) **Add time-to-value** (“2 minutes”, “5 minutes”, “today”)
4) **Mention the format** (Notion template / PDF / prompts)
5) **Avoid vague hype** (“ultimate”, “best ever”)
6) **Don’t promise money** (risk + low trust)
7) **Keep the first 1–2 lines clean** (no long headers, no fluff)

## Fast upgrade: write your first line as the snippet
Here’s a simple one-liner you can paste as the first line of your Gumroad description:

> **{Outcome} for {who}.** {Format}. Set up in {time}. Instant download.
